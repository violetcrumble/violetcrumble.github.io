about

I've been in the web development field for over 15 years. I've done freelance web design, worked at small agencies, and at large corporations. I'm currently a frontend software developer in a corporate environment.

Interested in more details about my professional experience?
Download my resume here or check out my LinkedIn profile.

I got into web design as a dorky teenager during the late 90s. I happened upon a print issue of the now defunct Yahoo! Internet Life magazine. The issue featured an article about basic HTML.

Armed with this article, a painfully slow 386 with a dial-up internet connection, and a free Geocities account, I started to experiment. I quickly fell in love with web design, since it allowed me to use both the creative and technical sides of my brain.

A month after graduating high school, I started classes at The Art Institute of Atlanta to study Interactive Media Design. While attending the Art Institute, I also worked for Coolblue Interactive, a web development agency in Atlanta.

Immediately after graduation from the Art Institute, I picked up a job at BellSouth Advertising and Publishing (now known as yellowpages.com.) I started out doing both graphic design and HTML/CSS for small brochure-type websites. These sites could be about anything from plumbers to lawyers to clowns - pretty much any business you would find in a phone book.

It was a great gig for a while, but six years later, it was time to move on. It was time for a change. In a strange twist of fate, I happened upon a web development position at Clayton Homes here in Knoxville, TN.

Reloacting to Knoxville and working at Clayton was a great decision! Here I am, ten years later, still enjoying all of the changes. I've also had the opportunity to work for Scripps Networks Interactive in Knoxville, with beloved brands like HGTV and Food Network. Although I enjoyed that position, Clayton and I could not live without each other, and I was invited to come back and work there again about a year and a half later. I actually get to experiment with new web development techniques at my job. Not to mention, I have a lot more spare time because I don't have to sit in traffic as much. This allows me to spend more time withe my very spoiled cat, Nermal. I also enjoy cake decorating, and running.
